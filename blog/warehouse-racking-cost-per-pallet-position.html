Warehouse Racking Cost per Pallet Position

2026 pricing breakdown by system type, component, and material — so you can budget with real numbers

← Back to Blog
Published: May 9, 2026 | Reading Time: 9 minutes | Author: RackingHub Team

In B2B racking procurement, "cost per pallet position" is the metric that actually matters. Quoting a full system price without knowing how many pallet positions it holds tells you nothing. A $40,000 rack system storing 800 pallet positions runs $50/position — competitive for selective racking. That same $40,000 storing only 600 positions is $67/position — a bad deal. This guide breaks down real warehouse racking costs by type, component, and the variables that move your final number up or down.

1. Cost per Pallet Position by Racking Type

These are 2026 FOB China price ranges for standard configurations — Q235B steel, powder-coated finish, 1,000–1,500 kg per beam level, 4–6 levels high. Prices shift with steel index fluctuations, so treat these as a baseline to work from.

Racking Type Cost per Pallet Position Density Level Best Application
Selective Pallet Racking $25 – $50 Standard Wide SKU range, 100% accessibility required
Drive-In Racking $20 – $40 High Few SKUs, high volume per SKU (beverages, paper)
Radio Shuttle Racking $60 – $120 Very High High-throughput cold storage, automated operations
Push-Back Racking $45 – $80 High Medium SKU count, LIFO acceptable
Double Deep Racking $30 – $55 Medium-High Balance between density and selectivity

Why does drive-in racking come in lower per position than selective? Because you eliminate aisles — fewer upright frames and beams per pallet position. The trade-off is reduced accessibility. For a deep dive into the trade-offs, see our comparison of pallet racking vs drive-in racking.

2. Component Cost Breakdown

When you receive a quote broken down by component, here's what each piece should cost in the current market. These are per-unit ranges for standard sizes:

Component Price Range Notes
Upright Frame $8 – $15 each Price depends on profile (80×80 to 120×95 mm) and steel thickness (1.5–2.5 mm)
Beam $6 – $12 each Length and gauge matter — 2.0m beams at 1.8mm steel sit mid-range
Wire Mesh Decking $5 – $10 each Required for non-standard pallet sizes or small carton storage
Diagonal & Horizontal Braces $2 – $4 each Per brace set — typically 2–3 sets per bay
Base Plate + Anchor Bolt $1.50 – $3 each M12 or M16 chemical anchors for concrete floors
Column Guard / Protector $15 – $35 each Installed at ground level in high forklift-traffic zones

Quick math example: A standard 2,700mm-wide bay with 5 levels uses 2 uprights, 10 beams, and 10 sets of bracing. Component cost alone lands around $180–$260 per bay — roughly 6 pallet positions per bay — putting you at $30–$43 per pallet position before decking or accessories. 3. Factors That Move Your Cost Up or Down

Steel Grade and Thickness

Q235B is the standard grade for warehouse racking in China. If you specify Q345B (higher yield strength at 345 MPa vs 235 MPa), you can use thinner profiles for the same load capacity — saving weight and cost — but the per-ton price is roughly 8–12% higher. Most projects don't need Q345B unless you're going above 10 meters or dealing with unusual seismic requirements.

Surface Coating

Powder coating is the default — good for dry indoor environments, costs roughly $0.30–$0.50/m² extra over bare steel. Hot-dip galvanizing is necessary for cold storage or humid coastal locations. It adds $0.80–$1.50/m² but prevents rust that would otherwise destroy your rack in 3–5 years. That upfront cost is cheaper than replacing corroded uprights.

Load Capacity per Level

Going from 1,000 kg to 2,000 kg per beam level typically means upgrading from 100×50 mm beams to 140×50 mm, and from 80×80 mm uprights to 90×70 mm or larger. Expect a 20–35% cost increase on components. But if your actual pallets weigh 1,800 kg and you spec for 1,000 kg, you've built a liability — not a savings.

Accessories

Column protectors, row spacers, wire decking, pallet supports, and aisle-end barriers all add cost but also extend the system's service life. Budget an additional 10–15% on top of the base racking price for a properly accessorized system.

4. FOB vs CIF Pricing — What Your Quote Actually Means

If you're importing racking from China, the Incoterm determines who pays for what. This is where many buyers get surprised by the final cost.

Term What's Included Buyer Pays
FOB (Free on Board) Product + inland transport to port + export customs Ocean freight, insurance, import duties, local delivery
CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) FOB + ocean freight + marine insurance to destination port Import duties, local port fees, delivery to warehouse

FOB pricing looks lower on paper, but once you add ocean freight ($800–$2,500 per 40HQ container depending on the route), insurance at 0.3% of cargo value, and port handling fees, your CIF-equivalent cost is typically 15–25% higher than the FOB quote. Always ask your supplier to quote both so you're comparing the same thing.

5. How to Reduce Total Cost of Ownership

Optimize the Layout Design

A layout designed around your actual pallet dimensions and forklift turning radius can cut pallet positions needed by 10–15%. Generic layouts waste space. For example: if your pallets are 1,100×1,100 mm but the design assumes 1,200×1,000 mm, you're paying for beam length and aisle width you don't need. Send your exact dimensions to the engineering team — it costs nothing and saves real money.

Order in Bulk, Phase Installation

If your warehouse will eventually need 2,000 pallet positions but you only need 800 right now, order the full 2,000-position material in one production run. You lock in the current steel price and save 5–8% on unit cost. Install what you need now, store the rest. Steel prices move — locking in a good rate is one of the most reliable ways to control cost.

Invest in Maintenance

The cheapest rack is the one you don't have to replace. EN 15635 requires daily visual inspections and annual professional audits. In practice, most rack failures come from unreported forklift impacts — a bent upright that goes unnoticed until the whole bay shifts. Install column guards at aisle ends, train operators on impact reporting, and keep spare uprights and beams on hand for immediate replacement. A $25 column protector prevents a $2,000 bay replacement.

6. Quick Cost Estimate Calculator

Here's a rough formula you can use to ballpark your project before getting a formal quote:

Estimate = (Number of pallet positions × Cost per position) × 1.15 (accessories + contingency)

Example: 500 pallet positions × $35 (selective racking) × 1.15 = ~$20,125 for material (FOB)

This doesn't include installation, freight, or import duties — but it gives you a number to work with in early-stage budgeting.
