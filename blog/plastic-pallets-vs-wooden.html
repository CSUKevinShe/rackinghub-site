Plastic Pallets vs Wooden Pallets: Which is Better for Your Business

A comprehensive comparison to help you choose the right pallet for your supply chain

← Back to Blog
Published: April 24, 2026 | Reading Time: 8 minutes | Author: RackingHub Team

Pallets are the unsung heroes of global logistics. Over 3 billion pallets are in use worldwide, and the vast majority are made from either wood or plastic. But which material is the right choice for your business?

The answer depends on your specific needs — budget, industry regulations, supply chain model, and sustainability goals. In this guide, we'll compare plastic and wooden pallets across every dimension that matters so you can make an informed decision.

1. Cost Comparison

Upfront Cost

Wooden pallets win on initial price. A standard wooden pallet costs $10–$30, while a comparable plastic pallet costs $30–$100+. For businesses that need hundreds or thousands of pallets, this upfront difference is significant.

Total Cost of Ownership

However, the story changes when you look at total cost of ownership. Plastic pallets last 10x longer than wooden pallets and don't require repairs. Over a 10-year period, plastic pallets often prove more economical, especially in closed-loop systems where pallets are tracked and returned.

Factor Wooden Pallets Plastic Pallets
Upfront cost $10–$30 $30–$100+
Lifespan 3–5 years 10+ years
Repair cost $2–$5 per repair Minimal to none
Weight 15–25 kg 5–15 kg

2. Durability & Lifespan

Plastic pallets are dramatically more durable. They resist moisture, chemicals, and impact damage. A well-made plastic pallet can withstand 200+ trips without significant degradation, while a wooden pallet typically lasts 20–40 trips before needing repair or replacement.

Wooden pallets are vulnerable to:

  • Moisture absorption leading to warping and rot
  • Splintering and nail protrusion
  • Insect infestation (requiring fumigation treatment)
  • Cracking under repeated stress

Key Insight: If your pallets travel through wet environments, outdoor storage, or refrigerated facilities, plastic pallets will dramatically outlast wooden alternatives.

3. Hygiene & Safety

This is where plastic pallets truly shine — and why they're mandatory in certain industries.

Food & Pharmaceutical Industries

Plastic pallets are non-porous, making them easy to clean and sanitize. They don't absorb moisture, bacteria, or contaminants. For FDA, HACCP, and GMP-compliant operations, plastic pallets are often the only acceptable choice.

Wooden Pallet Hygiene Concerns

Wood is naturally porous and can harbor bacteria, mold, and pests. Wooden pallets must be treated (heat-treated or fumigated) under ISPM-15 regulations for international shipping, adding cost and complexity.

4. Weight & Shipping Costs

Plastic pallets are significantly lighter — typically 30–50% lighter than equivalent wooden pallets. This matters for:

  • Air freight: Every kilogram counts. Lighter pallets mean lower shipping costs
  • Manual handling: Lighter pallets reduce worker fatigue and injury risk
  • Transport efficiency: More product weight, less pallet weight per shipment

5. Environmental Impact

The environmental debate is nuanced:

Wooden Pallets

  • Made from renewable resources (timber)
  • Biodegradable and compostable
  • Repairable and recyclable into mulch or particleboard
  • Lower carbon footprint in manufacturing

Plastic Pallets

  • Made from petroleum-based materials (HDPE or PP)
  • Recyclable at end of life (many manufacturers offer take-back programs)
  • Longer lifespan means fewer pallets manufactured overall
  • Reusable hundreds of times vs. dozens for wood

Verdict: If sustainability is your top priority and you have a repair/reuse program, wooden pallets have a slight edge. But plastic pallets' longevity and recyclability narrow the gap significantly, especially when made from recycled materials.

6. When to Choose Each Type

Choose Wooden Pallets When:

  • Budget is the primary concern
  • One-way or export shipping (pallets aren't returned)
  • Heavy loads (wooden pallets can handle 1,500+ kg static load)
  • High-friction surface needed for stability
  • Custom sizes needed quickly (wood is easier to customize)

Choose Plastic Pallets When:

  • Operating in food, pharmaceutical, or chemical industries
  • Closed-loop supply chain (pallets are tracked and returned)
  • Hygiene and cleanliness are critical
  • Automated warehouse systems (consistent dimensions matter)
  • Outdoor or wet environments
  • Weight reduction is important for shipping costs

Making the Right Choice

There's no universal winner — the best pallet depends on your specific operation. Many businesses use a hybrid approach: wooden pallets for one-way export shipments and plastic pallets for internal circulation and hygiene-sensitive applications.

The key is to evaluate your total cost of ownership, not just the purchase price. A plastic pallet that costs 3x more upfront but lasts 10x longer is the smarter investment for most closed-loop operations.
