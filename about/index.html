What is RackingHub?

RackingHub is a professional resource platform built for warehouse managers, logistics consultants, procurement specialists, and anyone involved in planning, specifying, or purchasing warehouse racking systems.

We provide free planning tools that generate layout diagrams, cost estimates, and bills of materials. We publish expert guides covering every major racking type, from selective pallet racks to very narrow aisle systems. And we maintain a curated directory of manufacturers, standards bodies, and industry resources to help you find the right information and the right suppliers.

Whether you are designing a new warehouse, expanding an existing facility, or simply researching your options, RackingHub gives you the knowledge and tools to make informed decisions.

What We Offer

Expert Knowledge Base

In-depth guides on selective racking, drive-in, VNA, cantilever, push-back systems — written by professionals with hands-on experience in racking design and international trade.

Free Planning Tools

Configure warehouse dimensions, select racking types, and generate instant layout diagrams with cost estimates and complete bills of materials — entirely in your browser.

Supplier Recommendations

Access our curated directory of vetted manufacturers and suppliers. Find the right partner for your project based on product type, region, and certification requirements.

Global Perspective

We cover standards and practices from Europe (FEM, EN), North America (RMI, ANSI), Asia-Pacific (AS), and the Middle East — because warehouse logistics is a global business.

Behind RackingHub

RackingHub is built and maintained by warehouse professionals with over 15 years of experience in pallet racking design, manufacturing, and international trade. Our team has worked on projects ranging from small retail backrooms to large-scale distribution centers for clients including IKEA, Qatar Airways, Goodyear, and WHO operations.

We created RackingHub because we saw a gap in the market: reliable, practical information about warehouse racking is scattered, sales-biased, or behind paywalls. We believe that better information leads to better warehouses — and better warehouses mean safer, more efficient supply chains.
