Drive-In Pallet Racking Systems for High-Density Storage

Maximize warehouse cube with LIFO racking engineered for bulk homogeneous goods

Request Quote WhatsApp

Drive-in racking is a high-density storage system built for warehouses that handle large volumes of a limited number of SKUs. Forklifts drive directly into the rack lanes to place and retrieve pallets on continuous rails, removing the need for individual aisles between every row. This LIFO system typically delivers 35-40% more usable storage volume compared to conventional selective pallet racking, making it one of the most space-efficient drive-in pallet racking configurations available.

Key Features

  • Up to 40% higher space utilization than selective racking by consolidating storage into deep lanes
  • LIFO inventory flow — last pallet loaded is the first pallet retrieved, suitable for non-perishable bulk goods
  • Forklift operates inside the rack structure on guide rails; no aisles required between storage lanes
  • Configurable lane depth from 2 to 10 pallet positions per lane, adjusted to your SKU turnover pattern
  • Load capacity of 500-1500kg per pallet position, determined by upright column thickness and beam section selection
  • Frame heights up to 12m with braced upright configurations for seismic and wind-load zones

Drive-In vs Drive-Through Racking

Drive-in racking and drive-through racking share the same basic structure but differ in access configuration. Drive-in systems have a single entry/exit point at the front of each lane, enforcing LIFO stock rotation. This arrangement maximizes floor space because only one working aisle is needed per block of lanes.

Drive-through racking opens on both ends of the lane, allowing forklifts to enter from one side and exit from the other. This enables FIFO (first-in, first-out) inventory management, which matters for perishable goods or products with expiration dates. The trade-off is a second working aisle, which reduces overall storage density by roughly 10-15% compared to a drive-in layout. Choose drive-in racking when your products are uniform and stock rotation order is not critical.

Applications

  • Cold storage and freezer warehouses — reduced refrigerated volume lowers energy consumption
  • Beverage distribution centers with high pallet counts of identical SKUs
  • Food processing facilities storing bulk ingredients or finished goods
  • Chemical warehouses handling uniform drum or IBC container loads
  • Dairy plants and packaging facilities with seasonal batch production
  • Any operation storing 5+ pallets of the same product per SKU

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between drive-in racking and drive-through racking?

Drive-in racking has a single entry point and uses LIFO (last-in, first-out) inventory flow. Drive-through racking has entry points on both ends, allowing FIFO (first-in, first-out) flow. Drive-in racks provide slightly higher storage density since they do not require a rear aisle.

How much more storage space does a drive-in system provide compared to selective racking?

A drive-in pallet racking system typically provides 35-40% more usable storage space than conventional selective pallet racking by eliminating most aisles and allowing deeper lane configurations of up to 10 pallets per lane.

What load capacity can drive-in racking support?

Standard drive-in racking supports 500-1500kg per pallet position. Actual capacity depends on upright frame gauge, beam section size, and lane depth. Our engineers calculate load specifications per project using FEM 10.2.02 design rules.

Is drive-in racking suitable for cold storage warehouses?

Yes. Drive-in racking is widely used in cold storage because the high-density layout reduces the volume of refrigerated space needed, lowering energy costs. We apply cold-resistant powder coating and specify steel grades suitable for sub-zero operating temperatures.

What safety standards does the drive-in racking comply with?

Our drive-in systems are manufactured to ISO9001:2015 quality management standards and comply with CE marking requirements and FEM 10.2.02/10.2.09 structural design specifications. We provide full structural calculation reports and installation guides with every order.
